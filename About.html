About

3 values: Drive, Simplicity, and Curiosity.

Drive

I learned to swim at 4, and swam competitively from 12 til 18 for Scarlet Aquatics and my high school team too. I was never the best, and the 3 hour/6 days a week practices killed me, but I left with a semi-athletic body and permanently competitive mindset that serves me well. Now, every once in a while I get the urge to exercise or work on a project/skill, and I can't rest until I satisfy that urge. To me, drive is the inexplicable urge to seek self-improvement, and I wouldn't be where I am today without it.

Simplicity

Computer science may be incredibly complicated on the lower levels, but thanks to abstractions, it is able to be simply understood by anyone. I chose CS as my major because it's the future of solving practical problems as efficiently as possible, but I'd also like to simplify what can sometimes seem like an impossible system into something accessible by anyone from anywhere. This could also be because I'm not smart enough to understand the complexities of CS, but let's go with this instead.

Curiosity

I'll be the first to say that I get bored very quickly about things I don't have any passion for. But at the end of the day, I know that knowledge is the foundation of all things, and if I am to be someone dependable, I have to be knowledgable. I already have a 4.0 GPA, but I want to seek knowledge through practical experience and repetition. For example, data science is a CS application that I want to look into in the future.
